Let's Talk

Artist Story

Body Odyssey began from a personal need to understand and speak about my own chronic illnesses, which I had kept private for many years due to internalised shame, stigma and a lack of open cultural conversation around invisible conditions. Early audio projects helped me explore my story safely and made me realise how many people felt the same. That recognition became the foundation for a wider creative exploration of how we talk about the body.

The project grew naturally from there. With The War Inside and Body Odyssey Live, both ACE funded, we saw how strongly audiences connected to the work. People wrote to us with their own experiences, often saying it was the first time they felt seen, and some travelled long distances to attend. Opportunities began opening in unexpected places: GP practices offered filming spaces, charities approached us to collaborate, and schools, universities and health organisations invited us to share the work. It became clear that these subjects were universal, underrepresented and deeply needed.

Body Odyssey is now a multi-strand project spanning books, performance, workshops and partnerships with charities and neurodivergent-led organisations. The vision is to continue building on this momentum, expanding collaborations across arts, science and learning spaces, and growing the project into a nationwide conversation. Our aim is to reach a wide range of communities and create imaginative, accessible tools that help people explore their bodies and health experiences with confidence, curiosity and care.

Camille Dawson, 2025
